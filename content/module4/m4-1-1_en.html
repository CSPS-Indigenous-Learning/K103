Mental health: suicide

The impacts of colonialism, socio-economic conditions and significant risk factors cause many young Inuit to lose hope and resort to suicide. Currently, the suicide rate of Inuit is among the highest in the world. It is 11 times the national average.

Self-reflection: How do you think this crisis has impacted families, communities and future generations?

Addressing the mental health crisis in Northern communities is a shared responsibility. Inuit Tapiriit Kanatami recommends a shared national, regional and community-wide response. It also recommends taking a holistic approach that covers the following areas:

  • creating social equity and cultural continuity
  • nurturing healthy children
  • increasing access to mental wellness services
  • addressing unresolved trauma and grief
  • mobilizing Inuit knowledge

The Government of Canada has a major role to play. This includes supporting mental health and suicide prevention programs and services. Programs are needed to raise awareness, improve knowledge and skills (of health care workers and community members), provide support for community-based programs and build resilience.
