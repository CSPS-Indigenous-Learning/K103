Contemporary realities

Loss of traditions

Historically, Inuit turned to the land and sea for their sustenance. Traditional foods included whale, walrus, caribou and seal. Inuit also gathered grasses, roots, berries and various types of seaweed for a balanced diet. They led very active lives and were constantly out on the land or maintaining the camps. There was little time for sedentary activities.

As Inuit started living in permanent settlements, many of the advantages of their traditional lifestyle diminished. There has been a decline in hunting for food, partly because young people lack the skills to survive off the land. The high cost of hunting equipment—snowmobiles, rifles, sleds, camping gear, gasoline and oil—is also a major factor.
