Chapter 2: Historical impacts of colonialism and legislation

The earliest European contact with Inuit likely happened in the 1500s when explorers, traders and whalers began visiting the Canadian Arctic. The Hudson’s Bay Company set up a post at the mouth of the Churchill River in 1717. After that, efforts to trade with Inuit increased.
