Inuit, a federal responsibility or not?

Inuit were not included in the 1876 Indian Act. For many years, responsibility for Inuit was transferred from one department to another or between federal and provincial governments.

In 1924, Inuit came to be considered Canadian citizens, rather than wards of the state like First Nations people. The Department of Indian Affairs was assigned responsibility for them.

In 1939, the Supreme Court of Canada pronounced its judgment on Reference Re Eskimos. Constitutionally, Inuit were to be classified as Indians in Canada under section 91(24) of the Constitution Act, 1867. The decision was based on the historical description of "Esquimaux" as an "Indian tribe" in numerous documents dating from 1760 to Confederation.

The Government of Canada now has jurisdiction to legislate on matters related to Inuit. This does not mean that Inuit have “status” like many First Nations individuals—and they do not fall under the Indian Act. Instead, the Government of Canada provides programs and services based on specific needs.
