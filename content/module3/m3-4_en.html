Question of

Inuit have banded together collectively into four regions to negotiate and sign comprehensive land claims agreements with the Government of Canada.


What are some of their concerns?

Answer: All of these.

Each treaty that was signed is unique and contains its own specific benefits and obligations. All of the treaties have implications for the Government of Canada. None of the agreements changed the fundamental citizenship of Inuit, who are all Canadians with the same rights and entitlements.

All Inuit are under a modern treaty except those living outside of Inuit Nunangat.

Answer: True.

The four regions of the Canadian Arctic—Nunavik in Quebec, Nunatsiavut in Labrador, Inuvialuit in the Northwest Territories and Nunavut—have distinct governance structures ranging from the public government of Nunavut, to the treaty provisions and municipal status of Nunavik in Quebec, to the slightly more limited comprehensive land claim in Inuvialuit. All of the regions have control over their own lands and work hard to determine their own futures.
