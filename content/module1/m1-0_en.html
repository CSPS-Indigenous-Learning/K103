Chapter 1: Getting to know Inuit

What’s in a name?

Inuit are the Indigenous peoples of the Arctic. The word Inuit means “the people” in the Inuit language of Inuktitut.

Until recently, people used the word “Eskimo”. However, in Canada, “Eskimo” is often considered a derogatory term. The respectful and more commonly used term is Inuit. The singular form of Inuit is Inuk, meaning person.

We use “Inuit” in this course except in the context of legislation and historical documents or in situations where the word “Eskimo” appears. The term “Aboriginal” will also come up in certain contexts such as quotes.

Inuit, as spelled out in Inuit writing syllabics, means “the people” in Inuktitut.

In English, the word “Inuit” should not be preceded by “the” or followed by “people” in common usage.
