Who are Inuit?

Inuit were here long before the creation of Canada and continue to thrive today. Inuit do not call themselves First Nations. Rather, they see themselves as a distinct group with their own culture, spirituality, language and know-how.

Languages

Inuit speak many different languages and dialects. However, all Inuit languages come from one main language family: the Inuit-Aleut or Eskimo-Aleut language family. The languages can be grouped into a Western branch and an Eastern branch, which are then further divided into other languages and dialects.

Western branch (Yupik languages)

Yupik is divided into three distinct languages:

  • Central Alaskan Yupik
  • Pacific Gulf Yupik (Alaska)
  • Siberian Yupik (Canada and Alaska)

Each of these three languages has several dialects.

Eastern branch (Inuktitut languages)

Eastern branch languages have three different names for the same language:

  • Inuktitut (in Canada)
  • Inupiaq (in Alaska)
  • Kalaallisut (in Greenland)

Many dialects of this language are spoken in the three countries.
