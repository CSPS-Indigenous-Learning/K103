The strongest Indigenous language in Canada

Inuktitut (also called Inuktut) is the language spoken by Inuit in Canada. This linguistic term encompasses all Inuit language groups and dialects spoken in Inuit Nunangat. Some of the dialects included are Inuttut, Inuinnaqtun and Inuvialuktun.

In the 2016 Canadian Census:

Approximately 57.3% of the total Inuit population reported Inuktitut as their mother tongue

Mother tongue

Mother tongue refers to the first language learned at home in childhood and that is still understood by the individual at the time of the census.
